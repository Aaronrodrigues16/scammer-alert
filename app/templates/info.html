Learn & Protect Yourself

Staying informed is your best defense against scams. Here's what you need to know about common scam tactics and how to protect yourself and your loved ones.

Common Scam Types

  • Phishing Scams: Fraudsters sending fake emails or texts impersonating legitimate organizations (banks, government agencies) to steal personal information.
    • Look for suspicious sender addresses, generic greetings, and urgent calls to action.
    • Never click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources.
  • Tech Support Scams: Pop-up warnings or calls claiming your computer has a virus, prompting you to call a fake "tech support" number. They'll try to gain remote access or demand payment.
    • Legitimate companies will not spontaneously call you about computer issues.
    • Never give remote access to unsolicited callers.
  • Romance Scams: Scammers create fake online profiles to build emotional relationships, then ask for money for emergencies, travel, or other fabricated needs.
    • Be wary of requests for money, especially if you've never met in person.
    • Scammers often avoid video calls or meeting up.
  • Grandparent Scams: A scammer pretends to be a grandchild in distress, needing money immediately for an emergency (bail, hospital bills, travel).
    • Always verify the story by calling the grandchild directly on a known number.
    • Ask personal questions only the real grandchild would know.
  • Investment Scams: Promises of high returns with little to no risk. Often involves cryptocurrency or fake investment platforms.
    • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
    • Research any investment opportunity thoroughly before committing funds.

General Protection Tips

  • Be Skeptical: Always question unsolicited calls, emails, or messages.
  • Verify Information: If contacted by someone claiming to be from an organization, find their official contact details independently and call them back.
  • Protect Personal Information: Never share sensitive details (passwords, bank details, Aadhaar/SSN) unless you initiated the contact and trust the recipient.
  • Strong Passwords & MFA: Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
  • Regularly Update Software: Keep your operating system, web browser, and antivirus software up to date.
  • Talk to Loved Ones: Educate family and friends, especially seniors, about common scams.
  • Report Scams: If you suspect a scam, report it to the authorities. Our tool is here to help you analyze and report!
